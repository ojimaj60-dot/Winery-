Our Heritage

A Legacy Poured
in Every Bottle

Wine is the only artwork you can drink.

For over a century, the Veloria family has cultivated the rich terroir of the Duero Valley. Each vine tells a story of patience, passion, and unwavering dedication to the craft of winemaking.

Our vineyards, planted at elevations above 900 metres, produce grapes of extraordinary concentration. The extreme continental climate forces the vines to dig deep, creating wines of remarkable complexity.

Our Story

Where Legacy
Meets Craft

Founded by Don Alejandro Veloria in 1892, our estate has been the custodian of some of Spain's most celebrated vineyards for five generations.

Today, under the stewardship of Elena Veloria, we blend ancestral wisdom with modern precision — honouring the past while crafting wines for the future.

1892

The Founding

Don Alejandro plants the first Tempranillo vines on the slopes of Sierra de Gredos.

1948

The First Reserva

Carlos Veloria produces the estate's first Reserva, aged 24 months in French oak.

1987

International Recognition

Veloria Gran Reserva earns 98 points from Wine Spectator, putting Spain on the world stage.

2019

A New Chapter

Elena Veloria introduces organic farming and carbon-neutral production across all vineyards.
